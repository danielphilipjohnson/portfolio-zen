## Interviewing Into the Void

At first, I thought: how bad can it be? I mean, I‚Äôve done video calls. I‚Äôm comfortable talking. I‚Äôm reasonably tech-savvy.

But there‚Äôs something deeply weird about smiling at your own reflection while trying to project confidence and warmth ‚Äî to no one. You‚Äôre expected to maintain eye contact, speak clearly, smile naturally. And you do. Because that‚Äôs what we‚Äôve trained ourselves to do in real conversations. You nod. You pause for reactions. ## No Feedback Loop, No Humanity

In a real interview, there‚Äôs a feedback loop. You say something, and maybe it doesn‚Äôt quite land ‚Äî so the interviewer asks, ‚ÄúCan you clarify?‚Äù or ‚ÄúI didn‚Äôt quite follow.‚Äù That little nudge helps you reframe your point, recover, even shine.

> ‚ÄúThere‚Äôs nothing like trying to build rapport with a loading icon.‚Äù

Honestly? It felt like being in a game of *Portal*. You're the test subject. The algorithm is GLaDOS. You keep talking, hoping you're getting it right, with no idea what ‚Äúright‚Äù even means.

At one point, I genuinely felt like I‚Äôd been taken captive and was recording a ransom plea.

‚ÄúPlease, let me work for your company. I have marketable skills. I can meet KPIs. Think of my family.‚Äù

## The Black Box with a Scorecard

It‚Äôs easy to laugh at the absurdity of AI interviews ‚Äî until you realise what‚Äôs really happening.

Behind the scenes, algorithms are evaluating your performance. Supposedly looking for things like communication skills, enthusiasm, and problem-solving ability. But how? Based on what? Says who ‚Äî the algorithm?

This is the heart of the **‚Äúblack box‚Äù problem** in AI hiring. HireVue, like many AI interview platforms, does not provide a transparent breakdown of how specific non-verbal cues ‚Äî facial expressions, vocal tone, body language ‚Äî are weighted. You‚Äôre assessed by unseen metrics, against undefined standards, with no opportunity to ask for clarification or feedback.

> ‚ÄúGetting feedback from an algorithm is like yelling into a canyon and hoping it explains your echo.‚Äù

It‚Äôs not just frustrating. It‚Äôs dangerous.

As a Forbes Tech Council article highlights, "In some cases, deeper AI learning models have become so advanced that even their creators don't fully comprehend how they work" \[1\]. That‚Äôs a terrifying sentence ‚Äî especially when your ability to land a job might hinge on whether or not you blinked too much.

> ‚ÄúIf the machine doesn‚Äôt know why it said no ‚Äî how are we supposed to learn from it?‚Äù

These systems are trained on historical data. And if that data reflects existing human bias ‚Äî around gender, race, age, or accent ‚Äî the system doesn‚Äôt remove bias. **It scales it** \[2\]. Amazon learned that the hard way with its now-retired AI hiring tool, which penalised CVs containing the word ‚Äúwomen‚Äôs‚Äù \[2\]. HireVue, for its part, publicly stopped using facial analysis after backlash \[3\]. But without full transparency into how their Natural Language Processing (NLP), sentiment scoring, or behavioural analytics work ‚Äî candidates are left guessing.

What should be a conversation becomes a monologue ‚Äî with no red pen, no context, no second chance. It‚Äôs the digital version of *‚ÄúComputer says no‚Äù* ‚Äî except this time, it‚Äôs your future on the line.

## Do They Know It‚Äôs Biased?

To be fair, HireVue and similar companies don‚Äôt pretend bias doesn‚Äôt exist. They claim to address it ‚Äî and to their credit, they‚Äôve moved beyond internal promises.

They‚Äôve brought in independent firms like DCI Consulting Group \[4\], ORCAA \[5\], and Landers Workforce Science LLC \[6\] to audit their systems. These are not subsidiaries. They‚Äôre not internal rebrands wearing a new logo and a trench coat.

But let‚Äôs be honest: if I pay someone to evaluate me, I‚Äôm at least hoping for a good review ‚Äî or at the very least, a polite one.

> ‚ÄúYou don‚Äôt hire the food critic to write the menu.‚Äù

Would you tell a billion-dollar client that their system might be unfair to disabled or neurodivergent candidates ‚Äî or that it subtly filters out people with regional accents ‚Äî if they‚Äôre the ones keeping your lights on?

> ‚ÄúTechnically independent. Financially dependent.‚Äù

Even the best-intentioned audits exist in a system of incentives. Consultants are still vendors. And vendors, as a rule, don‚Äôt bite the hand that signs the cheque.

And let‚Äôs be honest ‚Äî no one wants to upset the wrong billionaire or, heaven forbid, **Orange Man**. We might end up with a tariff on ‚Äúalgorithmic scrutiny‚Äù by morning.

We‚Äôve seen what happens when that kind of dynamic goes unchecked.

We‚Äôre not saying AI hiring will crash global markets. But when people‚Äôs livelihoods are being shaped by black-box systems reviewed by paid partners, the parallels are hard to ignore.

> ‚ÄúTrust doesn‚Äôt come from a contract. It comes from accountability.‚Äù

So if you're wondering why these tools keep getting deployed ‚Äî even with unresolved bias, vague metrics, and suspect audits ‚Äî maybe it‚Äôs not just the companies.

Maybe it‚Äôs because the **people building them aren‚Äôt being told to stop.** In fact, they‚Äôre being told to go faster.

## The Shifting Sands of AI Safety: A National Priority or a Nuisance?

The fight for ethical AI isn‚Äôt just happening in company town halls or consulting firm blog posts. It‚Äôs playing out at the highest levels of government. And the current message from the top? **Move fast, and break‚Ä¶ guardrails.**

Under the Trump administration, the U.S. has sharply shifted its AI priorities. Gone are the calls for ‚Äúresponsible AI‚Äù and fairness. In their place: a race to dominate global AI markets and root out so-called ‚Äúideological bias‚Äù \[8\].

Yes ‚Äî the *AI Safety Institute* is being told to stop talking about safety.

Instead, they‚Äôve been tasked with building tools to **‚Äúexpand America‚Äôs global AI position‚Äù** \[10\], following a January 2025 executive order aimed at eliminating anything seen as a ‚Äúbarrier to American AI innovation‚Äù \[10\].

> ‚ÄúSafety isn't a barrier to innovation. It‚Äôs the foundation for trust.‚Äù

Critics warn that stripping away these protections opens the door to unchecked, discriminatory algorithms ‚Äî including those making hiring decisions \[11\]. But proponents dismiss the concern. This isn‚Äôt just a policy change ‚Äî it‚Äôs a worldview shift. One that sees **friction as failure** and **guardrails as bureaucracy**. And it sends a clear message to companies building AI hiring tools:

You‚Äôre not just *allowed* to move fast.

So when you‚Äôre wondering why a platform like HireVue still feels like a digital interrogation booth with no feedback, no accountability, and no second chances ‚Äî maybe it‚Äôs because the people at the top decided that **speed wins.** Even if people lose.

## The Human Cost

I get it. Companies want efficiency. They want scalable hiring. They want to reduce bias. But somewhere in the pursuit of ‚Äúbetter,‚Äù we stripped away the human from Human Resources.

Real interviews are stressful ‚Äî but at least they‚Äôre a conversation. At least there‚Äôs nuance. A moment. A spark. HireVue replaces that with something colder. Something measurable. Something unaccountable.

And if you‚Äôre neurodivergent? The challenges are amplified. Time limits, lack of clarification, unclear expectations ‚Äî it‚Äôs a system designed around neurotypical behaviour \[13\]. If your accent doesn't match the model‚Äôs training data, or if your connection stutters, you‚Äôre already behind.

The ACLU recently filed a complaint against Intuit and HireVue, alleging their AI hiring technology works worse for deaf and non-white applicants \[14\]. This complaint highlights concerns that differences in speech patterns, accents, and communication styles can lead to biased outcomes. This isn't just about a "feeling" of unfairness; it's about demonstrable adverse impact on certain demographic groups, echoing concerns that have been raised by numerous studies on algorithmic bias in hiring.

> ‚ÄúEfficiency at scale sounds great ‚Äî until you realise you‚Äôve scaled dehumanisation.‚Äù

## For My Intern Friends: Surviving the Black Box

If you're staring down a HireVue interview and feeling the existential dread of talking to a blinking dot, here are a few tips to help you survive the void ‚Äî and maybe even score that callback.

**1\. Treat it like theatre, not a conversation.**

You won‚Äôt get nods, ‚Äúmm-hmms,‚Äù or follow-ups ‚Äî so front-load your clarity. Say what you mean, then say it again (briefly) with structure: "Here's the problem, here's how I approached it, here's what happened."

**2\. Practice with a timer and webcam.**

Simulate the awkward silence. I used the **Apple timer on my phone** ‚Äî set for 1‚Äì2 minutes ‚Äî then just stared at it while answering questions like I was defusing a bomb. It helped. No audience, no feedback ‚Äî just me, a screen, and the crushing awareness that somewhere, an algorithm was judging my vocal tone.

**3\. Smile ‚Äî but don‚Äôt force it.**

Yes, it‚Äôs weird. But a calm, confident demeanour helps signal ‚Äúcomposed and hireable‚Äù to whatever behavioural model is watching. Think *video cover letter*, not *hostage tape*.

**4\. Use STAR ‚Äî even when the question doesn‚Äôt ask for it.**

That‚Äôs *Situation, Task, Action, Result*. It gives your answer structure, which helps you stay on track ‚Äî and it helps the algorithm identify story arcs in your response.

**5\. Review real HireVue questions in advance.**

Sites like Coursera [üëâ this one](https://www.coursera.org/articles/hirevue-interview-questions) have lists of common questions by role. Don‚Äôt memorise answers ‚Äî but do prep your *go-to stories* for teamwork, problem-solving, leadership, and failure.

**6\. Learn from the greats (or pretend to).**

This [YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0at_B6RpKoY) taught me everything I needed to know: stare into the void, read a script, and *deliver it like you're leading a nation*. It's a teleprompter training video for aspiring anchors or presidents ‚Äî perfect prep for HireVue.

Honestly, after five sessions I felt ready to address the nation, launch a stimulus package, and maybe even explain my gap year with gravitas.

> ‚ÄúMy fellow stakeholders‚Ä¶ in Q4, I overcame adversity by aligning cross-functional synergies under tight deadlines.‚Äù

Honestly, if the next U.S. election isn‚Äôt run through HireVue, it‚Äôs a missed opportunity. At least then we‚Äôd have a fair metric for comparing the real candidates to Mark Zuckerberg ‚Äî At least then we‚Äôd all get to see which candidates blink too much under pressure.

> ‚ÄúCandidate A: Answered clearly. Blinking rate: suspiciously low.‚Äù

Let‚Äôs face it: we‚Äôre not being judged like humans. We‚Äôre being scored like deepfakes trying to pass a CAPTCHA.

> *‚ÄúI did not have inappropriate pauses with that question.‚Äù* ‚Äî You, to the algorithm, 2025

**7\. And if all else fails?**

Remember: this isn‚Äôt the final boss. It‚Äôs just one gatekeeper.

You‚Äôre more than your sentence flow, your vocal tone, or how long you paused before answering.
